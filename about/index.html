About Me

Hi, I’m Max Wasserman. I enjoy all forms of disciplined movement (weight lifting, gymnastics, yoga), meditation, and using engineering principles to build useful things. I grew up in suburban New Jersey, spent a few years in Philadelphia for undergrad, and now find myself in Rochester, NY for my PhD advised by Dr. Gonzalo Mateos.

I currently work on projects attempting to extend machine learning tools such as Deep Neural Networks to graph structured data. My works lies at the intersection of (graph) signal processing, deep learning, and uncertainty estimation. Previously, I worked as a Robotics Engineer at the GRASP lab in Philadelphia on topics relating to robotic autonomy with Dr. Dan Lee. Before that I worked on Point-of-Care diagnostics in the Issadore Lab.
